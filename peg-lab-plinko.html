Peg Lab Plinko!

An interactive STEM learning toy for 4th and 5th graders — designed, fabricated, and programmed end-to-end by a 5-person team I co-led for Cornerstone of Engineering 2.

Overview

The brief: design a STEM learning toy that 4th and 5th graders would actually want to play with, and that could teach something along the way. Our team landed on Peg Lab Plinko! — a Plinko-style board where the peg layout and lighting respond to player input, turning the classic dropped-puck mechanic into a small physics lesson.

What I did

  • Co-led a 5-person team through concept, prototyping, and final build over the semester.
  • Designed mechanical components in SolidWorks and fabricated parts via laser cutting and 3D printing.
  • Built and programmed Arduino-based electronics in C++ for the interactive behavior — sensors, lighting, and player feedback.
  • Delivered monthly design reviews and incorporated peer and mentor feedback into successive iterations of the toy.

What I learned

Designing for kids forces clarity. If a 4th grader can't figure out what to do in the first ten seconds, the engineering doesn't matter. Most of the late-stage iteration was about removing friction from the play experience, not adding more features.

On the team side, this was my first real exposure to running monthly design reviews — preparing the work, taking critique, deciding what to act on, and moving forward. That cadence has carried over into how I work in AerospaceNU now.
